Attendance Policy for Student Success and Retention

The College expects all enrolled students to attend classes regularly and to participate in instructional activities in a manner consistent with course expectations. Attendance is treated as an early indicator of academic engagement and is reviewed in conjunction with course performance, advising notes, and student support referrals. Students who miss class repeatedly, especially in the first six weeks of a term, may be identified for outreach and intervention by faculty, academic advisors, or the Student Success Office.

When a student demonstrates a pattern of excessive absences, the college will initiate a structured support process. This process may include outreach from an academic advisor, a reminder of available tutoring and counseling resources, and a review of any barriers affecting attendance, such as transportation, health concerns, housing instability, or family responsibilities. Students with documented circumstances may receive temporary accommodations or referral to appropriate campus support offices.

Attendance-based intervention thresholds will be applied consistently across programs, with additional attention given to first-year students, transfer students, and students on academic notice. The purpose of this policy is not punitive, but preventive. The college seeks to ensure that students receive timely support before attendance difficulties become persistent academic concerns. Continued nonattendance after intervention may result in further administrative review and possible impact on course standing or financial aid eligibility, depending on institutional policy and applicable regulations.
