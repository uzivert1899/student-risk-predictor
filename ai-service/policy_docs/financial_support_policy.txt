Financial Support and Tuition Accountability Policy

The College is committed to supporting students who experience financial hardship that may affect enrollment persistence, course completion, or timely registration. Students with outstanding tuition balances, delayed payment status, or unresolved financial holds may be referred for financial counseling and support planning. The institution encourages students to engage early with the Office of Student Financial Services whenever they anticipate difficulty meeting payment deadlines or maintaining eligibility for continued enrollment.

In cases where a student is not current on tuition payments, staff may review the student's account status, discuss available payment plans, and identify potential funding resources such as emergency grants, institutional aid, or external scholarship opportunities. Students who hold scholarships are expected to maintain any required academic standards and may be reviewed for renewal or adjustment if performance or eligibility criteria are no longer met. The College also reserves the right to evaluate exceptional circumstances, including documented medical issues, family emergencies, or involuntary changes in employment.

Support interventions will be handled with discretion and in accordance with institutional procedures. Students who are not up to date on tuition payments may be directed to financial advising, payment negotiation options, and referral to campus resources such as the food pantry, emergency assistance fund, or case management services. The College aims to prevent financial barriers from becoming the primary cause of withdrawal or noncompletion.
