Grade Decline and Academic Support Policy

The College recognizes that academic performance can fluctuate across semesters due to changing life circumstances, course difficulty, adjustment to college expectations, or competing responsibilities. For this reason, students whose grades decline significantly from one term to the next will be reviewed for academic support as part of the institution's retention framework. A meaningful grade decline is defined as a marked reduction in course performance, especially when accompanied by withdrawal activity, repeated low assessments, or difficulty completing major assignments.

When a decline is identified, the student will be contacted by an academic advisor or designated support staff member for a proactive conversation. The purpose of this review is to identify contributing factors, clarify academic expectations, and connect the student with services such as tutoring, supplemental instruction, study strategy coaching, and faculty office-hour support. Students may also be referred to disability services, learning support, or counseling if academic difficulties are linked to broader personal or emotional challenges.

Faculty and academic support personnel are expected to document concerns promptly and share them with the appropriate advising team when intervention may be beneficial. These support measures are intended to promote persistence and timely recovery. Students who do not engage with support resources after a documented decline may be placed on academic monitoring, and repeated patterns of decline may trigger additional review by the academic standing committee.
