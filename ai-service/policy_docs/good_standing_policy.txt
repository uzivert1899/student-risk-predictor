Good Standing and Academic Recognition Policy

The College recognizes students who demonstrate strong academic progress and consistent engagement in their coursework. Students who maintain solid grade performance across consecutive terms, complete a high proportion of attempted units, and show steady progress toward degree requirements may be identified as being in good standing and may receive institutional recognition. This recognition may include academic commendation, inclusion in student success communications, eligibility for certain honors programming, or referral to leadership and scholarship opportunities when appropriate.

Consistent grade performance is generally defined as maintaining satisfactory or above-satisfactory academic results, with no sustained pattern of failing or incomplete work that would suggest significant risk to degree progress. High unit completion rates refer to the successful completion of a substantial share of enrolled coursework within the term, with particular attention to students who are progressing at a pace consistent with their academic plan.

A student is considered “on track” when they are meeting expected milestones for their program, including timely course completion, progress toward required credits, and no unresolved barriers that would materially delay graduation. This status is intended to support positive reinforcement and to encourage continued persistence, especially for students who are improving after earlier academic difficulty.
