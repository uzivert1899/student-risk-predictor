General Counseling and Student Support Referral Policy

The College provides general academic counseling to support student persistence, goal clarification, and successful progression toward degree completion. Counseling services are available to students who are experiencing difficulty adjusting to college expectations, managing academic demands, navigating major requirements, or balancing school with family or work responsibilities. Students may request counseling services directly, or they may be referred by faculty, advisors, or support staff when concerns are observed in academic performance, engagement, or personal well-being.

When a student presents with persistent difficulties that exceed the scope of routine advising, the College may refer the student to appropriate campus resources such as counseling services, disability support, wellness programs, career development, or basic needs assistance. Referral decisions will be made thoughtfully and in accordance with the student's stated needs, the seriousness of the concern, and the availability of campus services. Staff members are expected to maintain confidentiality and to document significant concerns in a manner consistent with institutional guidelines.

The purpose of general counseling is to provide early, supportive intervention that helps students build academic resilience and connect with resources before problems escalate. Students who decline support or do not respond to outreach may be monitored through standard academic support channels, and repeated concerns may prompt additional review by advising or student affairs leadership.
